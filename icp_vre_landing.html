Rationale

ICP monitoring data move through several critical stages before they can support scientific interpretation or European reporting: field and laboratory templates, physicochemical validation, outlier review, database ingestion, satellite-derived indicators and final environmental analysis. Weaknesses at any stage can propagate through the chain and reduce the reliability, comparability and reusability of the final evidence.

This project treats the complete data lifecycle as a single reproducible pipeline. Each workflow transforms raw or intermediate inputs into versioned outputs, preserving traceability from the original sample, plot or area of interest to the final analytical product. The aim is not only to validate chemistry, but to connect laboratory data, spatial information and long-term indicators within one transparent processing system.

Hypothesis & Project Relevance

The central hypothesis is that ICP data become more robust, scalable and useful when the full processing chain is automated as a connected system rather than handled as isolated tasks. Linking validation, anomaly detection, database update, satellite processing and environmental analysis can reduce delays, increase consistency and make outputs easier to audit, reproduce and compare across plots and years.

  • Complete data journey — from raw templates and AOIs to final analytical outputs
  • Traceable decisions — quality flags, outlier labels, database records and index layers
  • Integrated evidence — combining chemistry, monitoring metadata and satellite indicators
  • Modular design — each workflow reusable independently or as part of the full pipeline
  • FAIR-oriented outputs — versioned ZIP files, structured data and reproducible reports
